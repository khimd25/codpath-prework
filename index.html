Khim David began her journey not behind a desk, but behind a camera — producing stories for Arizona PBS that turned raw footage into something audiences actually cared about. Early quests taught her the most important skill of all: translate complexity into clarity.

When Apple came calling, she discovered that the same craft applied to enterprise. Multi-stakeholder lifecycle transformations are just 60-minute documentaries with more Slack channels. Over six years and seven roles, she mastered people operations, editorial planning, red-team engineering, and financial services strategy. Few adventurers cross that many disciplines without losing the thread. She never did.

Her greatest power is a rare one: she speaks Finance, IT, Ops, and Exec fluently — not as a translator, but as a native. She builds bridges between silos not because she was trained to, but because she genuinely sees no walls. A first-gen Filipino-American lens that values both rigor and humanity.

Now she seeks a new quest — one where operational excellence meets human impact. A place where the work of enablement: training people, clearing friction, scaling support, directly changes lives. That quest is called Lyra Health.
